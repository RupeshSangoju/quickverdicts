# Quick Verdicts — Juror User Guide

**For:** Jurors using the Quick Verdicts platform
**Covers:** Account creation → onboarding → applying to cases → trial → verdict

---

## What Is Quick Verdicts?

Quick Verdicts is an online mock jury trial platform. As a juror you:

- Apply to virtual mock trial cases posted by attorneys
- Get selected (or not) by the attorney reviewing applications
- Prepare in a War Room before the trial
- Participate in a live virtual trial
- Submit your verdict after deliberation

Everything happens online — no courtroom, no travel. You are compensated for your time based on the case tier.

## 1. Creating Your Account (Sign Up)

Go to the Quick Verdicts website and click **Sign Up → Juror**.

The sign-up process has **4 steps**. Your progress is saved automatically.

---

### Step 1 — Eligibility Questions

Before anything else, you must answer eligibility questions to confirm you qualify for jury service. Answer each question honestly with **Yes** or **No**.

The questions cover:
- Whether you are 18 years of age or older
- Whether you are a U.S. citizen
- Work-related availability questions
- Whether you have a prior felony conviction
- Whether you are currently under indictment

> **Important:** If your answers indicate you do not meet the eligibility requirements (e.g., under 18, not a citizen, or currently under indictment), you will not be able to proceed with registration.

Click **Next** when all questions are answered.

---

### Step 2 — Personal Details

This step has two parts.

**Part A — Your Information:**

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Full Name | Required — letters, spaces, hyphens, and apostrophes only. No numbers or special characters. |
| Occupation | Your current occupation |

Click **Next** to continue to Part B.

**Part B — Contact & Payment:**

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Address Line 1 | Street address (required) |
| Address Line 2 | Apartment, suite, etc. (optional) |
| State | Select from dropdown (required) |
| County | Populated automatically after selecting a state (required) |
| City | Populated automatically after selecting a state (required) |
| ZIP Code | 5-digit or 9-digit ZIP (required) |
| Phone Number | 10-digit US number in (XXX) XXX-XXXX format (required) |
| Payment Method | How you want to be paid — choose one: **Venmo**, **Zelle**, or **Personal Check** |

Click **Next** when complete.

---

### Step 3 — Email & Password

**Part A — Set your credentials:**

| Field | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Email | Valid email address not already registered |
| Password | At least 8 characters, one uppercase letter, one number, one special character, no more than 2 identical characters in a row |

After clicking **Next**, a **6-digit verification code** is sent to your email.

**Part B — Verify your email:**

Open your email, find the code, and enter it on screen. If it didn't arrive, click **Resend Code**. Once verified, click **Next**.

---

### Step 4 — User Agreement

Read the full User Agreement. Scroll to the bottom to enable the checkbox, then check **"I agree to the terms"** and click **Create Account**.

Your account is created and you are logged in automatically.

> **Note:** Your account starts as **Pending Verification**. An admin will review your registration. You will gain full access to the Job Board once verified. In the meantime you can complete your onboarding (video and quiz).

## 2. Logging In

Go to the website and click **Login → Juror**.

Enter your **email address** and **password**, then click **Sign In**.

If you forgot your password, click **Forgot Password** and follow the reset instructions sent to your email.

The platform automatically logs you out after **20 minutes of inactivity**.

## 3. Onboarding — Video & Quiz

When you log in for the first time (or until onboarding is complete), the platform requires you to complete two tasks before you can apply to cases. These are shown as progress cards on your Home screen.

---

### Task 1 — Introduction Video

Click **Watch Video** on the onboarding card.

A video overlay appears covering the main content area. Your sidebar remains accessible.

**Video rules:**
- You **cannot skip forward** — seeking ahead is disabled
- You **can pause and resume** at any time
- The progress bar is read-only (you cannot click it to jump ahead)
- The video is approximately 5 minutes long

Once the video finishes, the **Continue to Quiz** button appears at the bottom right. Click it to proceed.

> If you close the overlay before the video finishes, your progress is not saved. You will need to watch it again from the beginning next time.

---

### Task 2 — Juror Quiz

The quiz opens automatically after you complete the video, or you can click **Start Quiz** from the Home screen (the quiz is locked until the video is done).

The quiz has **8 questions** covering how the Quick Verdicts platform works. Each question must be answered before moving to the next.

After answering each question the correct answer and an explanation are shown. Work through all 8 questions and click **Finish** on the last one.

**What happens:**
- Your quiz completion is recorded
- Onboarding is marked complete
- The Job Board unlocks (once your account is also verified by admin)

> The onboarding section disappears from your Home screen once both tasks are finished.

  - Status badge and the action button for that status (see table below)

- A preview of open cases on the job board

## 5. The Job Board — Finding Cases

Click **Job Board** in the sidebar to browse all cases currently open for applications.

**What you see per case card:**

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Case name | Plaintiff v. Defendant (e.g., "Smith v. Jones") |
| Case ID | Unique case identifier |
| Attorney / Law Firm | Who posted the case |
| Spots available | How many jurors are still needed |
| Trial Date | When the trial is scheduled |
| Time | Start time of the trial |
| Location | County where the case is filed |
| Compensation | How much you will be paid for this case |

**Compensation by tier:**

| Tier | Your Pay | Trial Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Early Adopter | $50 | ~2.5 hours |
| Tier 1 | $75 | ~2.5 hours |
| Tier 2 | $100 | ~3.5 hours |
| Tier 3 | $125 | ~4.5 hours |

**Search and sort:**
- Type in the search box to filter by case name or attorney
- Use the **Sort** dropdown to order by:
  - Trial Date (Earliest first)
  - Trial Date (Latest first)
  - Pay (Low to High)
  - Pay (High to Low)

Click **Apply** on any case to start your application.

> **Locked state:** If your account is not yet verified, the entire Job Board shows a lock message. You cannot apply until the admin approves your account.

## 6. Applying to a Case

Click **Apply** on a case card to open the application page at `/juror/apply/[case-id]`.

---

### Case Summary

At the top of the page you will see:
- Case name and ID
- Law firm name
- **Compensation amount** (shown prominently in green)
- Trial date, time, and location
- Case description (written by the attorney — general information about the type of case)

---

### The Application Form

The application is the attorney's **Voir Dire** — a series of questions used to screen jurors. There are two parts.

**Part 1 — Standard Qualification Questions**

These are the same for every case on the platform. There are 7 questions covering potential bias, personal interest in the case outcome, family involvement, health issues, and ability to be impartial. All are **Yes / No** questions.

A green checkmark appears on each question card once you answer it.

**Part 2 — Case-Specific Questions**

These are custom questions written by the attorney for this particular case. They may be:
- **Yes / No** — click Yes or No
- **Text Response** — type your answer in the text box provided

Answer all questions honestly. The attorney will review your responses when deciding whether to approve or reject your application.

**Progress bar:**

A progress bar at the top of the page shows how many questions you have answered (e.g., "8 / 10 questions answered"). The **Submit Application** button only activates when all questions are answered (100%).

---

### Submitting

When all questions are answered:
1. Review your responses
2. Click **Submit Application**
3. A loading spinner shows briefly, then a success toast appears
4. You are returned to your dashboard

> If you change your mind, click **Cancel** to go back without submitting.

---

## 7. After You Apply — Waiting for Selection

After submitting, your application status appears on your Home screen under **My Applications**.

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending Attorney Approval** | The attorney has not reviewed your application yet |
| **Approved — War Room Access** | You have been selected! Your War Room is now open. |
| **Not Selected** | The attorney declined your application for this case |

You will receive a **notification** when the attorney makes a decision.

If approved: a **War Room** is created for you for that case. Access it from your Home screen or by navigating to the case.

If not selected: your application is closed. You can apply to other cases on the Job Board.

## 8. The War Room (Trial Preparation)

The War Room is your preparation space for an approved case. Access it from your Home screen — find the case card and click **Case Information**.

---

### Case Details

At the top of the War Room you see:
- Case name and ID
- Your compensation amount
- Trial date and time
- County and state

---

### Case Documents

Any documents the attorney has uploaded for the case appear here. These may include exhibits, evidence summaries, case background materials, and other reference files.

**To view a document:**
- Click on its name
- A viewer opens inside the page

Supported formats:
- **PDF** — displayed in an embedded PDF viewer
- **Word / Excel** — displayed via Office Online viewer
- **Text files** — displayed as plain text
- **CSV / Excel spreadsheets** — displayed as a table
- **Video** — played with a built-in video player

---

### Witnesses

A list of witnesses who will testify during the trial. Each entry shows:
- Witness name
- Which side they represent (Plaintiff or Defendant)
- A brief description or background

Review these so you know who will be speaking during the trial.

---

### Team Members

Lists the attorneys and staff involved in the case — plaintiff's counsel and defense counsel — with their names, roles, and contact details.

---

### Jury Charge

The jury charge contains the legal instructions you will follow when deliberating and reaching a verdict. It is written by the attorney.

**Before the trial:** The jury charge may show as *"Not yet released"* — the attorney will release it when ready.

**Once released:** The full jury charge text is displayed for you to read and review before the trial.

> Read the jury charge carefully before trial day. It explains exactly what you need to decide and the legal standards that apply.

---

### Case Rescheduling

If the attorney reschedules the trial:
- You receive a **notification**: *"The case '[Case Name]' has been rescheduled to [new date] at [new time]. Your application has been removed. You can reapply from the job board if you are available at the new time."*
- Your application is removed from the case
- The case returns to the Job Board at the new date
- You can reapply if you are available for the new date

## 9. Trial Day

### Before You Join

On the day of your trial, make sure you:

1. Log in to Quick Verdicts at least **10 minutes early**
2. Have your camera and microphone working
3. Have a stable internet connection
4. Are dressed in **business or business casual** attire
5. Are in a quiet, distraction-free location
6. Have reviewed the case documents and jury charge in your War Room

---

### Joining the Trial

From your Home screen, find the case card. On trial day it shows a **Join Trial** button (it pulses green to get your attention).

Click **Join Trial** to enter the virtual courtroom.

---

### Inside the Trial

The trial runs as a virtual video conference. You will see and hear the attorneys presenting their case.

**Your role during the trial:**
- Listen attentively to both sides
- Do not speak unless instructed
- Keep your camera on and stay visible
- Mute your microphone when you are not speaking
- Do not record the session — this is legally prohibited

**Trial duration** depends on the case tier (2.5 to 4.5 hours). Stay for the full duration.

---

### If You Are Selected as Presiding Juror

One juror is randomly selected to be the **Presiding Juror**. If that is you, you will see a notification in the trial interface. The Presiding Juror:
- Helps guide the jury's discussion during deliberation
- Enters the jury's collective answers into the verdict form on behalf of the group

---

## 10. After the Trial — Submitting Your Verdict

After the trial presentations end, the jury deliberates privately. You will then need to submit your verdict through the platform.

---

### Accessing the Verdict Form

Go to your War Room for the case (Home screen → case card → **Case Information**).

The **Verdict Form** appears in the War Room when:
- The jury charge has been released by the attorney
- It is trial day or the trial has already taken place

---

### Completing the Verdict

The verdict form contains the specific questions you need to answer based on the jury charge instructions. Answer each question as instructed.

Once all questions are answered, click **Submit Verdict**.

- You will see a confirmation that your verdict has been recorded
- The attorney and admin are notified of the submission

**If you are the Presiding Juror:** You enter the group's answers on behalf of all jurors. Make sure the responses reflect what the jury decided collectively during deliberation.

---

### After Submission

- Your case status changes to **Trial Completed**
- The case card on your Home screen moves to a completed state
- You can still view the case details and documents in your War Room

**Payment:** Your compensation is processed based on the payment method you selected during sign-up (Venmo, Zelle, or Personal Check).

## 11. Your Profile & Account Settings

Click **Profile** in the sidebar to manage your account.

---

### Viewing Your Information

Your profile shows:
- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- State and county
- Password (shown as dots — the actual password is never displayed)
- Your payment method (Venmo, Zelle, or Personal Check) — shown with a green checkmark

> **Note:** Your payment method is set during sign-up and is displayed here for reference only. To change it, contact support.

---

### Editing Your Profile

Click **Edit Profile** to update your information.

**Editable fields:**
- Full Name
- Phone Number

**Email** cannot be changed from the edit form.

**Changing your password:**
1. Enter your new password in the password field inside Edit Profile
2. A verification code is sent to your registered email
3. An OTP prompt appears — enter the 6-digit code
4. Click **Verify and Update**
5. Your password is changed and your profile is saved

If you are not changing your password, leave the password field blank and click **Save** — your name and phone are updated directly without OTP.

---

### Deleting Your Account

> ⚠️ This is permanent. Once deleted your account is deactivated and cannot be recovered through the UI.

1. In the Profile section, find **Manage Account**
2. Click **Delete Account** (shown in red)
3. A confirmation dialog appears
4. Enter your **current password** to confirm you are the account owner
5. Click **Delete**

**What happens:**
- Your account is deactivated immediately
- You are signed out and redirected to the login page after 2 seconds
- Your case history and payment records are retained in the system for administrative purposes
- You can no longer log in

If you deleted your account by mistake, contact Quick Verdicts support as soon as possible.

## 12. Notifications

Click the **bell icon** in the sidebar (or select **Notifications**) to see your notifications. An unread count badge appears on the bell when you have new notifications.

---

### Types of Notifications You Receive

| Event | What the notification says |
|---|---|
| Application approved | The attorney selected you for a case |
| Application rejected | The attorney did not select you for this case |
| Case rescheduled | Your case has a new trial date; your application has been removed |
| Case cancelled | The case you applied to has been cancelled |
| Trial reminder | Reminder that your trial is coming up (24 hours before, 1 hour before) |
| Payment update | Information about your compensation |
| System announcements | Platform updates or important notices |

---

### Managing Notifications

**Mark as read:** Click on any notification to mark it read.

**Mark all as read:** Click **Mark All Read** at the top of the list.

**Delete a notification:** Click the trash icon on any notification card.

**Delete multiple:** Check the boxes on several notifications, then click **Delete (X)**.

**Filter:** Use the **All / Unread** buttons to show all or only unread notifications.

Notifications are shown 50 per page. Use the page buttons at the bottom to navigate older ones.

## 13. Common Questions

**Q: I signed up but can't see any cases. Why?**
A: Two things must happen before you can access the Job Board: (1) an admin must verify your account, and (2) you must complete the onboarding video and quiz. Check your Home screen — it will show what is still pending.

**Q: How long does account verification take?**
A: Typically 1–2 business days. You will receive a notification when your account is verified. Until then you can complete the onboarding video and quiz.

**Q: I watched the intro video but it won't let me proceed. Why?**
A: You must watch the full video without skipping. The Continue button only appears when the video has played all the way to the end. Make sure you did not close the overlay before it finished.

**Q: Can I apply to more than one case at a time?**
A: Yes. You can apply to multiple cases simultaneously. Each application is independent.

**Q: I applied to a case and got approved, but now the case shows as rescheduled and my application is gone. What happened?**
A: When a trial is rescheduled, all juror applications are automatically removed so new jurors can apply for the new date. You will receive a notification explaining this. If you are still available for the new date, you can reapply from the Job Board.

**Q: What if I can no longer attend a trial I was approved for?**
A: Contact Quick Verdicts support as soon as possible. Dropping out of an approved case without notice affects the attorney's trial. Do not simply stop responding — notify the platform.

**Q: Do I need any legal knowledge to be a juror?**
A: No. Quick Verdicts jurors are regular members of the public, just like a real jury pool. The attorney will explain everything during the trial. Your job is to listen fairly and return an honest verdict.

**Q: When do I get paid?**
A: Payment is processed after the trial is completed. It is sent to the payment method you selected during sign-up (Venmo, Zelle, or Personal Check). Contact support if you have questions about a specific payment.

**Q: Can I change my payment method after signing up?**
A: The payment method shown in your Profile was set during sign-up and cannot be changed through the app. Contact Quick Verdicts support to request a change.

**Q: What does the Presiding Juror do?**
A: The Presiding Juror is randomly selected from the jury. They help guide the jury's discussion during deliberation and are responsible for entering the group's verdict answers into the verdict form on behalf of the whole jury. You will be notified during the trial if you are selected.

**Q: I missed my trial. What do I do?**
A: Contact Quick Verdicts support immediately and explain what happened. Missing a trial without notice may affect your ability to participate in future cases.

**Q: How do I know when the trial is about to start?**
A: You will receive reminder notifications 24 hours before and 1 hour before your scheduled trial. On trial day, your Home screen shows a pulsing green **Join Trial** button on the case card.

**Q: Can the attorney see my voir dire answers before selecting me?**
A: Yes. The attorney reviews all applicants' answers to the qualification and case-specific questions before deciding who to approve or reject. Answer all questions honestly.
