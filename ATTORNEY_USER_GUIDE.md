# Quick Verdicts — Attorney User Guide

**For:** Attorneys using the Quick Verdicts platform
**Covers:** Account creation → case submission → trial → completion

---

## What Is Quick Verdicts?

Quick Verdicts is an online mock jury trial platform. As an attorney you can:

- Create a virtual mock trial case
- Have real jurors apply and get selected
- Prepare your case in a dedicated War Room
- Run a live virtual trial
- Receive the jury's verdict

Everything happens online — no courtroom, no travel.

## 1. Creating Your Account (Sign Up)

Go to the Quick Verdicts website and click **Sign Up → Attorney**.

The sign-up process has **4 steps**. Your progress is saved automatically so you can leave and come back without losing what you filled in.

---

### Step 1 — Personal Details

Fill in your professional information:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| First Name | Required |
| Middle Name | Optional |
| Last Name | Required |
| Law Firm / Entity Name | The name of your law firm or practice |
| Phone Number | Must be unique — not already registered |
| State | The state where you hold your bar license |
| State Bar Number | Your official bar number for that state |

Click **Next** when complete.

---

### Step 2 — Office Address

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| State | Select from dropdown (this is your office location) |
| County | Populated automatically after you select a state |
| City | Populated automatically after you select a state |
| Office Address Line 1 | Street address |
| Office Address Line 2 | Suite, floor, etc. (optional) |
| ZIP Code | 5-digit ZIP |

Click **Next** when complete.

---

### Step 3 — Email & Password

**Part A — Enter your credentials:**

| Field | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Email | Must be a valid email address, not already registered |
| Password | At least 8 characters, one uppercase letter, one number, one special character, no more than 2 identical characters in a row |

After you click **Next**, a **6-digit verification code** is sent to your email.

**Part B — Verify your email:**

Open your email and enter the 6-digit code on screen. The code expires after a few minutes. If it didn't arrive, click **Resend Code**.

Once verified, click **Next**.

---

### Step 4 — Terms & Agreement

Read the User Agreement. Scroll to the bottom, then check the box **"I agree to the Terms & Conditions"** and click **Create Account**.

Your account is created and you are automatically logged in.

> **Note:** Your account starts as **unverified**. The Quick Verdicts admin team will review your bar number and contact information. You will receive an email once verified. Some features are limited until verification is complete.

## 2. Logging In

Go to the website and click **Login → Attorney**.

Enter your **email address** and **password**, then click **Sign In**.

If you forgot your password, click **Forgot Password** and follow the instructions sent to your email.

## 3. Your Dashboard

After logging in you land on your **Dashboard**. The left side has a navigation sidebar:

| Section | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Home** | Overview — stats, upcoming trials, quick actions |
| **Cases** | All your cases, filterable by status |
| **Calendar** | Visual calendar of scheduled trials |
| **Notifications** | Alerts about case approvals, jurors, trial reminders |
| **Profile** | Your account information and settings |
| **Sign Out** | Log out of your account |

### Verification Status

A badge in the sidebar shows whether your account is **Verified**, **Pending**, or **Rejected**.

- **Pending** — Admin is reviewing your credentials. You can still explore the platform.
- **Verified** — Full access. You can create and run cases.
- **Rejected** — Check the rejection reason in your Profile and resubmit any required documents.

## 4. Creating a New Case

From your dashboard, click **"Create New Case"** on the Home section.

This opens a **7-step wizard**. Each step is saved automatically — if you navigate away, your data is preserved.

---

### Step 0 — Choose Jurisdiction

Select whether your case is:

- **State** — State court jurisdiction
- **Federal** — Federal court jurisdiction

> At this time, Quick Verdicts serves **Texas jurisdictions**. Click your selection to proceed.

---

### Step 1 — Case Details

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| **State** | Select the state where the trial venue is located |
| **County** | Select the county (updates automatically based on state) |
| **Type of Trial** | Fixed: "Civil (Standard)" — cannot be changed |
| **Case Tier** | Choose the tier that matches your time and budget needs (see below) |
| **Number of Jurors** | Fixed: Minimum 6, Maximum 8 — cannot be changed |
| **Case Description** | Describe your case for the juror job board. **Do not include confidential information** — jurors will read this before applying. |

#### Case Tiers

| Tier | Trial Duration | Attorney Cost | Juror Pay |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Adopter | 2.5 hours | $2,000 | $50 |
| Tier 1 | 2.5 hours | $3,500 | $75 |
| Tier 2 | 3.5 hours | $4,500 | $100 |
| Tier 3 | 4.5 hours | $5,500 | $125 |

The tier you select determines how long your trial session runs and how much jurors are compensated.

Click **Next** when complete.

---

### Step 2 — Plaintiff Details

Enter information for the plaintiff side. You can have **multiple plaintiff groups** if there are multiple opposing counsel scenarios.

**For each Plaintiff Group:**

**Mock Legal Representatives** (the attorneys representing plaintiffs in the mock trial):
- Name (required)
- Email (required) — used to notify them

You can add multiple representatives per group using **"+ Add Mock Legal Representation"**.

**Plaintiffs** (the parties being represented):
- Name (required)

You can add multiple plaintiffs per group using **"+ Add Plaintiff"**.

To add an entirely separate plaintiff group (different counsel, different plaintiffs), click **"+ Add Another Plaintiff Group"**.

Click **Next** when complete.

---

### Step 3 — Defendant Details

Same structure as Plaintiff Details but for the defendant side.

**For each Defendant Group:**

**Mock Legal Representatives:**
- Name (required)
- Email (required)

**Defendants:**
- Name (required)

Use **"+ Add Another Defendant Group"** to add multiple defendant groups.

Click **Next** when complete.

---

### Step 4a — Default Voir Dire Questions

This screen shows **7 standard disqualifying questions** that are always asked to every juror applicant. These are pre-set by Quick Verdicts and cannot be edited:

1. Do you know or recognize any of the parties involved in this case?
2. Have you or an immediate family member been involved in a similar dispute?
3. Do you have any personal or financial interest in the outcome of this case?
4. Do you have any bias for or against any of the parties?
5. Is there any reason you cannot render a fair and impartial judgment?
6. Do you have any health, time, or personal issues that would prevent full participation?
7. Can you base your decision solely on the facts presented and the law as instructed?

Review these, then click **Next**.

---

### Step 4b — Custom Voir Dire Questions

Add case-specific questions to further screen jurors. This step is optional but recommended.

For each question:

| Field | Options |
|---|---|
| **Question Text** | Type your question |
| **Response Type** | "Yes / No" or "Text Response" |

- **Yes / No** — Juror answers with a simple yes or no
- **Text Response** — Juror provides a written answer

Click **"+ Add Another Voir Dire Question"** to add more questions.

These questions are shown to jurors during their application process. Their answers will appear in your War Room for review.

Click **Next** when complete.

---

### Step 5 — Payment Details

Select your **Payment Method**:

- Credit Card
- Debit Card
- Bank Transfer

If you select Credit Card or Debit Card, enter:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Cardholder Name | Letters and spaces only |
| Card Number | 16 digits — formatted automatically |
| Expiry Date | MM/YY format |
| CVV | 3 digits |

The **Payment Amount** is shown automatically based on your chosen tier (from Step 1). This amount is fixed and cannot be changed.

Click **Next** when complete.

---

### Step 6 — Review Details

A summary of everything you've entered is displayed:

- State, County, Jurisdiction, Tier
- Case description
- All plaintiffs and their representatives
- All defendants and their representatives
- Payment method and amount

Review carefully. If something needs to change, use your browser's back button to go to the relevant step.

When everything looks correct, click **"Pay & Schedule Trial"**.

---

### Step 7 — Schedule the Trial

This is the final step — pick the date and time for your trial.

**Selecting a Date:**

A calendar shows the current and future months. Each date is color-coded:

| Color / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Normal (clickable) | Available — click to select |
| Red with ✕ | Fully blocked by admin — cannot be selected |
| Orange with warning | Partially blocked — some time slots unavailable |
| Gray | Past date — cannot be selected |

Click on an available date.

**Selecting a Time:**

After selecting a date, time slots appear in 30-minute intervals for the full day (midnight to 11:30 PM). Unavailable slots (admin-blocked or already past) are hidden automatically.

Click your preferred start time.

**Confirming the Booking:**

A summary panel appears showing:
- Trial Date (e.g., Monday, May 19, 2026)
- Trial Start Time and estimated End Time (based on your tier duration)
- Location (County, State)
- Format: Virtual Trial

Enter your **Name** and **Email** to confirm, then click **"Confirm scheduling request."**

> ✅ **Your case has been submitted!**
>
> You will see a message: *"Trial date pending QV confirmation. War Room will be accessible upon confirmation of trial setting."*
>
> You are then redirected to your dashboard.

## 5. After You Submit — Waiting for Approval

After submitting your case, it enters **Admin Review**.

What happens:
1. The Quick Verdicts team reviews your case details
2. Your case appears in your **Cases** section with status: **Pending**
3. Jurors can see your case on the job board and begin applying
4. You will receive a **notification and email** when your case is approved or if changes are needed

**Case Statuses:**

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Pending | Submitted — awaiting admin approval |
| Scheduled | Approved — trial date is confirmed |
| In Progress | Trial is happening now |
| Completed | Trial has ended |
| Cancelled | Case was cancelled |

## 6. The War Room (Trial Preparation)

Once your case is **approved**, a **War Room** is created for it. This is your preparation hub where you manage everything before the trial.

To access: Go to **Cases** → find your case → click **"View War Room"**.

---

### War Room — Case Overview

At the top of the War Room you'll see:
- Case title (auto-generated as "Plaintiff v. Defendant")
- Case ID, Status, Tier
- Scheduled date and time
- County, State, Jurisdiction

---

### War Room — Juror Applications

This tab shows all jurors who have applied to your case.

Each juror entry displays:
- Juror name
- Application status: **Pending**, **Approved**, or **Rejected**
- Their answers to the **default voir dire questions** (Step 4a)
- Their answers to your **custom voir dire questions** (Step 4b)

**Actions:**
- **Approve** — Accept this juror for your trial
- **Reject** — Decline this juror

You need a minimum of 6 approved jurors to proceed with the trial. A maximum of 8 are seated.

> **Tip:** Review voir dire answers carefully. Jurors who answered "Yes" to disqualifying questions (e.g., bias, personal interest) should typically be rejected.

---

### War Room — Documents

Upload any documents that will be used or shared during the trial.

**To upload a document:**
1. Click the upload area or drag and drop a file onto it
2. Add a description for the document (e.g., "Exhibit A — Medical Records")
3. The file uploads and appears in your document list

**Managing documents:**
- View all uploaded files with name, description, and upload date
- Download any document
- Delete a document you no longer need

Supported file types include PDF and common document formats.

---

### War Room — Witnesses

Add mock witnesses who will testify during the trial.

For each witness enter:
- Name
- Title / Role (e.g., "Expert Witness — Medical")
- Brief description

Witnesses can be added or removed at any time before the trial starts.

---

### War Room — Jury Charge

The jury charge contains the legal instructions the jury will receive before deliberating.

- Write or paste your jury charge text
- Release the charge to jurors when ready
- Track whether the charge has been released

## 7. Trial Day

### Before You Join

1. Be logged into Quick Verdicts at least **10 minutes early**
2. Ensure your camera and microphone are working
3. Have a stable internet connection
4. Be dressed in **business or business casual** attire
5. Be in a quiet, professional environment

### Joining the Trial

From your dashboard or Cases section, find your case and click **"View Trial"**.

**Best Practices Screen**

You will first see a list of virtual courtroom best practices. Read through them, check **"I agree"**, and click **Continue**.

**Technical Setup**

A setup screen lets you test your camera and microphone before entering. Confirm everything works and proceed.

**Live Trial (Conference Room)**

You are now in the virtual courtroom. The interface includes:

| Control | Function |
|---|---|
| Microphone button | Mute / unmute yourself |
| Camera button | Turn video on / off |
| Screen Share | Share your screen with participants |
| Chat | Send messages to participants |
| Documents | Share uploaded case documents |
| Raise Hand | Signal you want to speak |

The panel on the side shows:
- Case name
- Attending jurors
- Current witness (if applicable)
- Time elapsed

> ⚠️ **Recording is prohibited.** Do not record the trial session. This is a legal requirement stated in the platform terms.

### During the Trial

Run your mock trial as you would in any courtroom setting:
- Give opening statements
- Present evidence and exhibits (share via Documents)
- Question witnesses
- Make objections
- Give closing arguments

Jurors observe, take notes, and will deliberate privately after proceedings conclude.

---

## 8. After the Trial

Once the trial session ends:

1. Your case status changes to **Completed** in your dashboard
2. The jury deliberates (privately, off-platform)
3. A verdict is returned and posted in your case details
4. You will receive a **notification** when the verdict is available

**Viewing the Verdict:**

Go to **Cases** → select your completed case → view the verdict details including:
- The jury's decision
- Juror breakdown (if available)
- Any juror feedback

The completed case remains in your Cases section for your records.

## 10. Common Questions

**Q: How long does account verification take?**
A: Typically 1–2 business days. You'll receive an email when your account is verified.

**Q: Can I edit my case after submitting?**
A: Some details can be edited from the War Room before the trial starts. Contact support if you need to make major changes.

**Q: What if I need to reschedule my trial?**
A: Contact Quick Verdicts support through the Help section on your dashboard. Reschedule requests must be submitted before the trial date.

**Q: How many jurors do I need?**
A: A minimum of 6 approved jurors are required. The platform seats up to 8. Juror applications open as soon as your case is posted.

**Q: What if not enough jurors apply?**
A: Quick Verdicts will notify you. Your trial date may need to be rescheduled to allow more jurors to apply.

**Q: Can jurors see confidential case information?**
A: No. Jurors only see your **Case Description** (which you write in Step 1) and your **Voir Dire questions**. Do not put confidential information in the case description.

**Q: What happens to my payment if the case is cancelled?**
A: Contact Quick Verdicts support for refund or credit inquiries. Payment policies are detailed in the Terms & Conditions you agreed to during signup.

**Q: Can I run multiple cases at once?**
A: Yes. Each case is independent and managed separately. All your cases appear in the **Cases** section of your dashboard.

**Q: Who are the jurors?**
A: Jurors are real people registered on the Quick Verdicts platform who have passed eligibility screening. They are not legal professionals — they represent the perspective of a real jury pool.

**Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?**
A: On the login page, click **Forgot Password**, enter your registered email, and follow the reset instructions sent to your inbox.
